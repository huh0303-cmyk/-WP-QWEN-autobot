Here you can set up custom redirections. It is important to choose the right type of redirection.

  • 301 redirections are permanent. The old URL will be removed in search engines and replaced by the new one, passing on SearchRank and other SEO scores. Browsers may also store the new URL in cache and redirect to it even after the redirection is deleted from the list here.
  • Using a 302 temporary redirection is useful when you want to test a new page for client feedback temporarily without affecting the SEO scores of the original page.
  • Redirections can be exported to your .htaccess file for faster redirections, in SEO > Settings > Import/Export.

You can customize the display of this screen's contents in a number of ways:

  • You can hide/display columns based on your needs.
  • You can decide how many items to list per screen using the Screen Options tab.
  • You can search items using the search form at the top.
  • You can reorder the list by clicking on the column headings.

Hovering over a row in the list will display action links that allow you to manage the item. You can perform the following actions:

  • Edit redirection details: from/to URLs and the redirection type.
  • Activate/Deactivate redirections. Deactivated redirections do not take effect on your site.
  • Delete permanently removes the redirection.

You can also activate, deactivate, or delete multiple items at once using the Bulk Actions dropdown.
